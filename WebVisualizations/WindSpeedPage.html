Wind Speed versus Latitude




Temperature differentials observed in the WeatherPy data suggested the potential for atmospheric disturbances across geographical regions, which would manifest as wind currents. Much as was observed for the humidity data, no obvious correlation between wind speed and latitude were observed. However, a small possible correlation with higher wind speed and higher and lower latitude may be significant, as evidenced by almost no measured wind speeds above 8 MPH within +/-20 degrees of the Equator, but multiple measurements in excess of 10 MPH at latitudes above and below this Equatorial range. One potential limitation of this study is the question if differences in latitude temperatures produced spcific direcctionality of air currents. However, this study did not measure the directionality of wind currents necessary for such analysis.
